💌 How to set up a signature for all your emails in Outlook

If you want your email signature to appear on all your emails in Outlook, follow the steps below:

The easiest way to create an email signature is to prepare it in Microsoft Word first. It is much easier to set up and format before copying it over to Outlook. You are welcome to use my template document if you would like: Email signature template

Feel free to customize your signature however you like. In this example, I am using a table with invisible outside borders, an image in the left cell, and text in the right cell. I have also added "Kind regards" right above the table.

Once you are happy with the result, open the document containing your brand-new signature.
Select everything (you can use the Ctrl + A shortcut or your mouse). Press Ctrl+ C to copy the signature to your clipboard.

Go to your email inbox and click the gear icon in the top right corner to open Settings.

Type "Signatures" in the settings search box and click on the result.

Click the blue Add signature button

Name your signature (e.g., "Main").
Paste your signature from the clipboard (press Ctrl + V or right-click and select Paste).
You will see a preview of how your signature will appear in your emails. Please check the default settings below before saving your changes.

Do not forget to press Save button

Done! Now you have your new signature added to Outlook

😨🆘 I am getting annoying alerts and notifications from Virus, Antivirus, unknown websites

If you are getting unexpected notifications and pop-ups about in your browser, please do not click on any links or buttons within them. They usually contain warnings about viruses and security problems—such as claiming your hard drive is infected—but don't worry, these are completely fake!

I will give you example below:

While they are harmless until you click on them, they can be quite annoying. The best way to deal with them is to remove any suspicious websites from your notification list. Once you do that, they will stop appearing.

Since we use the Microsoft Edge browser, you will need to open Edge settings. Here is how to do it:

Click on the 3-dots menu in the top right corner and select Settings.
You can also press Alt + E to open the menu and click Settings from there.

Next, follow this path to get to your Notification Settings:
Privacy, search, and services -> Site permissions -> All permissions -> Notifications

Here, you will see a list of all websites that have permission to send you notifications. Scammers often abuse this feature to try and scare you. You can easily remove any websites from this list that you do not recognize or do not need.

It is perfectly fine to clear the entire list if you aren't sure. Any legitimate websites (like Outlook or Calendar) will simply ask for your permission again the next time you visit them. Moving forward, just be cautious when allowing notifications or other permissions while surfing the web.

⚠️Important: If you feel unsure at any step, or if you accidentally clicked on one of those alert messages, please feel free to contact IT support or email IT Engineer directly. We will be more than happy to help you!

🖱️ Change colour, size, speed of your mouse pointer (cursor)

If you want to adjust your mouse cursor or movement speed, you can easily do so by following this guide.

First, open the Settings app on your device. You can find it by opening the Start menu and typing "Settings" into the search box, or by simply pressing the WIN + I shortcut.

To change the color and size of your cursor, navigate to Bluetooth & devices in the left menu. Here, you will find all your connected devices. Click on Mouse (or Touchpad if you primarily use a laptop touchpad, though you can adjust both). Once you click on Mouse, you can customize several settings to your liking

  • Primary mouse button: Swapping this can be highly useful for left-handed users.
  • Mouse pointer speed: This controls how fast your pointer moves across the screen.
  • Enhance Pointer Precision: This setting applies mouse acceleration. Because it alters your cursor speed based on how fast you physically move your mouse, it can make movements inconsistent and hinder your ability to build muscle memory.

In Scrolling section, you can adjust your scrolling behavior, which will affect both your mouse wheel and touchpad scrolling (two-finger scrolling). If you need to adjust specific touchpad gestures, you can always go back to Bluetooth & devices and select Touchpad.

To change the size or color of your pointer, go to Accessibility in the left menu, then select Mouse pointer and touch. In this menu, you can choose your preferred pointer style, size, and custom color.

You can also explore other helpful options here, such as the Mouse indicator. This feature displays a circle around your cursor every time you press the CTRL key, which is incredibly useful if you ever lose track of your pointer on the screen. Feel free to explore these settings to find the perfect setup for you!
