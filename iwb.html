Whiteboard's (Interactive screens) troubleshooting

If you are experiencing any issues with your whiteboard—such as a black screen, a "No Signal" message, an unresponsive touchscreen, or sound problems—please follow the troubleshooting steps outlined below.
📽️ Check Project options in Windows

Your screen may not display anything if your laptop is currently set to work with only one monitor. This applies to both Windows 10 and Windows 11. You can easily change this to the "Duplicate" or "Extend" option by pressing Windows Win + P, as shown below.

💡Quick Tip: Just click on the option you need:

  • Duplicate: This will show the same desktop on both screens. This is the most common choice.
  • Extend: This will create two separate desktops across your screens.
📶 Check Source signal on your screen

Cycle through the inputs using the Source (or Input) button until your desktop appears. There may be different ways to access this menu, as the Source (or Input) button could be located on the screen's bezel or an external control panel.

💡Quick Tip: Menus may look different from screen to screen, but they should always be easy to find on your home screen. Please try to locate the source (input) settings button.

⚠️ Please ensure that you have tested all available HDMI source options, as your device could be connected to any one of them.

⚡ Is Whiteboard powered ON?

All screens have a power button and often include a mode indicator; typically, a red LED signifies standby mode, while a green LED indicates the power is on. Please make sure your screen is turned on.

The whiteboard may also have a power switch located behind the screen. This is usually an I/O toggle switch; please ensure it is set to the "I" position.

🔌 Check your cables

Problems with your screen may be caused by disconnected cables. Setup for a screen, docking station, laptop, or desktop can vary, but the main difference is whether or not you are using a docking station. Please check your options below.

I use a Docking Station

Dock stations can look different but thay all works same way. Check the USB-C cable connecting your laptop to the dock.

This type of device does not require any special settings for use. This device may or may not have a power indicator. Regardless, please ensure all connections are secure to confirm it is functioning properly. Please gently push them in to make sure they are fully connected.

Dockstation can have 2 types of usb connection. It can be USB-C or USB-A. Be sure you are using USB-C if you have USB-C type dockstation and USB-C port in you laptop

Please ensure that one of those cables is plugged into your laptop on one end and into the docking station on the other.

I Don't use a Docking Station

If your setup does not include a docking station, you only need to check two things: the HDMI cable, which provides the screen image, and the USB cable (usually USB-A), which enables the touchscreen functionality.

This is a great way to rule out any issues with a faulty port.

🔲 Checking power sockets

Your laptop, desktop, monitor, docking station, and whiteboard screen may be plugged into different sockets or power strips. It can sometimes be tricky to locate all of these power connections, but please ensure they are all securely plugged in and that any individual power switches on the sockets are turned on.

🔇 Any sound issues

Occasionally, you may encounter audio issues with our boards. If the sound remains silent even after checking the cables and restarting the device, please try the following troubleshooting steps:

Check the Screen Volume. First, ensure that the screen is not muted (🔇) and that the volume level is not set to "0." You can adjust the volume using the 🔉 (Volume Down) and 🔊 (Volume Up) buttons located on the screen's frame. These buttons may look slightly different depending on your screen's manufacturer and model.

Check the Browser Tab. Next, check the status of your browser tab. Regardless of which browser you use, it is possible for a specific website to be muted. To fix this, right-click on the browser tab at the top of your screen and select the "Unmute tab" or "Unmute site" option. Edge and Chrome examples.

Open System Settings. Check the sound settings on your device. You can open the settings menu by pressing Win + I or by selecting the gear icon from the Start menu🪟.

Navigate to System > Sound and find the Output section.

⚠️ If nothing helped (full setup reset)

Sometimes you just need to reset your setup. Often, when the exact cause of a problem is unclear, the best approach is to restart everything.

To perform a full reset, please follow these steps for your devices:

  • Your laptop or desktop computer: Ensure you have fully shut down your computer. It should not be in sleep mode or simply signed out; it needs to be completely powered off.
  • Your docking station: If you have one, it may not have a power button. You can power it down by unplugging it from the wall or turning off the power at the socket.
  • Your interactive screen: You can turn this off by using the I/O power switch. If you cannot find the switch, please turn off the power at the socket or unplug the power cable.

💡Quick Tip: For a complete restart, it is best to power off all devices at once. We recommend waiting 60 seconds before turning them back on. You may turn your devices back on in any order.
